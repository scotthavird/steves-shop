The Craftsman

It Starts With
the Hunt

For Steve, every board begins long before the workshop. It starts with the hunt — scouring local sawmills, visiting lumber yards, and chasing down leads on fallen trees with character. He's looking for that perfect slab: the walnut with cathedral grain, the spalted maple with lightning-bolt veining, the burl with figure you can't stop running your hand across.

Finding the right wood is half the joy. The other half is transforming it — shaping, sanding, and finishing each piece until it becomes something beautiful enough to display, yet rugged enough to use every single day. Every board is sealed with a food-safe finish and branded with Steve's signature logo — his mark of craftsmanship.

The Wood

Steve works with over ten species of hardwood — each chosen for its color, grain, and character. Here's what makes each one special.

Walnut

Rich, dark chocolate brown with flowing cathedral grain. The backbone of the collection.

Maple

Creamy white with subtle figure. Creates beautiful contrast against darker woods.

Cherry

Warm pinkish-red that deepens beautifully with age. A timeless American classic.

Purpleheart

Vibrant, saturated purple from Central and South America. Unmistakable in any combination.

Yellowheart

Bright golden yellow that adds warmth and pop. A natural mood-lifter in any board.

Mahogany

Rich reddish-brown with distinctive ribbon grain. Warm, deep, and endlessly elegant.

Wenge

Near-black with dramatic coarse grain texture. Brings bold contrast and visual weight.

Padauk

Vivid orange-red that ages gracefully to a deep burgundy. Show-stopping color.

Lacewood

A unique lace-like spotted pattern unlike any other wood. A rare find that collectors seek out.

Spalted Maple

Maple transformed by fungi into a canvas of dark lines and organic patterns. Nature's ink work.

The hunt for the right piece of wood is just as rewarding as what I make from it. Every slab has a story — where the tree stood, how it fell, what the grain reveals. My job is to let that story come through.
From Forest to Table

How It's Made

Every board goes through a deliberate, hands-on process. No shortcuts, no mass production — just patient craftsmanship.

01

The Hunt

Scouring sawmills, lumber yards, and salvage sites for wood with character, figure, and soul.

02

The Cut

Milling raw lumber to precise thickness, reading the grain to maximize beauty in every piece.

03

The Build

Gluing, clamping, and shaping — sometimes with multiple exotic species arranged for maximum contrast.

04

The Finish

Progressive sanding to silky smooth, food-safe oil finish, and Steve's signature brand burned into every board.
